# ✈️ Aerospace Engineering Research — Aerodynamics & Airfoil Studies

> An independent research repository focused on experimental aerodynamics, airfoil optimization, and lift/drag characterization.

---

## 👤 About the Researcher

Early undergraduate pursuing a degree in Aerospace Engineering with a concentrated focus on aerodynamics and fluid behavior. This repository documents hands-on experimental work conducted independently — from concept and design through fabrication, testing, and data analysis. The goal is to bridge academic theory with real, measurable results while building a foundation for advanced research in lift/drag optimization and airfoil performance.

---

## 🔬 Research Focus

This repository serves as the central hub for all aerodynamics research projects, experimental data, documentation, and findings. The work here is organized around three core areas:

**Airfoil Design & Optimization**
Investigating how geometric parameters — camber, chord length, angle of attack — affect aerodynamic performance across different flow regimes.

**Lift vs. Drag Characterization**
Quantifying the lift-to-drag ratio under varying test conditions to identify optimal operating ranges and performance trade-offs for different airfoil profiles.

**Flow Behavior Under Varying Conditions**
Studying how airflow transitions, separates, and reattaches under changes in velocity, pressure, and geometry — with a focus on understanding boundary layer dynamics through experimental observation.

### 2. 🔄 Airfoil Comparative Study
**Status:** In Progress / Planned  
A systematic comparison of multiple airfoil profiles (symmetric vs. cambered, thin vs. thick) tested under identical conditions. Results will include lift/drag curves, stall angle data, and performance rankings.

> **Coming:** Raw test data, comparative graphs, and analysis writeups for each profile tested.

### 3. 🧪 Drag Reduction Experiments
**Status:** Planned  
Iterative experiments exploring surface modifications, geometry adjustments, and configuration changes aimed at reducing drag without sacrificing lift. Each iteration will be documented with before/after data.
